AstroPlan Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

AstroPlan is an astrophotography planning app. This policy explains, plainly, what the app does and does not do with your data.

Location

AstroPlan uses your device's location to calculate which deep-sky objects are visible from where you are, and when they rise, transit, and set. This calculation happens entirely on your device. Your location is never transmitted anywhere, never stored on a server, and never shared with any third party. If you save a location by name (e.g. "Home" or a dark-sky site), that saved location is stored only in the app's local database on your device.

No accounts, no tracking

AstroPlan does not require an account, does not collect analytics, and does not use advertising or tracking identifiers of any kind. There is no data about you to lose, sell, or share, because none is collected.

In-app purchases

AstroPlan offers optional, entirely voluntary tips to support development. These purchases are processed by Apple's App Store (StoreKit); AstroPlan never sees or stores your payment information.

Third-party data used to build the app

AstroPlan's deep-sky object catalog, images, and popularity rankings are drawn from public astronomical sources (OpenNGC, the Sharpless catalog via VizieR/CDS, Wikipedia, and the Digitized Sky Survey) and bundled into the app at build time. Full attribution is listed in the app's About screen. Using the app does not send any request to these sources — all of this data ships inside the app itself.

Children's privacy

AstroPlan does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children, because it does not collect personal information at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the update will be posted on this page with a revised date above.
